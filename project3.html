Introduction

Human activities each day require energy and nutrients that the body obtains from consuming meals. Knowing how to choose appropriate foods according to the body's needs helps it to grow fully, thrive, and maintain robust physical health. The creators therefore conceived the idea to develop a project for designing and developing a game to learn about proper nutritional choices. With the aim of imparting nutritional knowledge in the form of Game-based learning, the goal is for players to easily understand and enjoy the learning process.

Core Concept

My Health Meal is a single-player life simulation game where players control a character from start to finish. Players navigate through various locations with different mini-games designed to educate them about making proper nutritional choices. They can assess the correctness of their daily food consumption.

Players must apply nutritional knowledge to progress through the game, with different skills embedded in each mini-game. These skills include basic nutritional decision-making abilities applicable in everyday life. Players can continuously play to practice and refine their skills in making informed nutritional choices.

Main Features

1. Story


Restaurant


Quiz

My Healthy Meal consists of 2 Mini games, Restaurant and Quiz, respectively. When the game starts, players will be able to choose which Mini game to play.

In the mini-game 'Restaurant,' players start with 5 hearts. They can choose from 10 food items in sequence, selecting up to 5 dishes maximum. Each time a food item is chosen, the hearts do not decrease. They can also skip or pass on up to 5 dishes. Each time a dish is skipped or passed, 1 heart is deducted. The game randomly presents a total of 15 food items, each categorized as sweet, oily, or salty. When a player selects a food item, the corresponding energy tube increases. Players see the summary of sweet, oily, and salty energy tubes at the end after selecting the 10th food item.

In the mini-game 'Quiz,' players must answer 10 true/false questions. Each correct answer earns 1 point, while each incorrect answer earns 0 points. At the end, a summary of the player's food consumption choices is displayed.

8. Game Flow

When entering the game, players will encounter a screen displaying the game's name with buttons for Start, Exit, and Games and Rules. Game rules can be viewed by clicking on the Games and Rules button.

Press the Next button to view the rules of the next game.

Click "Back to main menu" to return to the initial screen upon entering the game. Players should click on the "Start" button to begin the game and proceed to various location screens.

When passing through the Restaurant location, which is the first place to enter the mini-game, a blue "Play" button will appear. Then you press the blue "Play" button, you will enter the Restaurant game screen, which starts with 5 pink hearts. The screen will display the first food dish for players to decide whether to choose or not. If they choose to select it, they should press the green "Select" button; if they decide not to choose it, they should press the red "Pass" button.

The "Select" and "Pass" buttons can be pressed up to 5 times each. If pressed 5 times already, from the 6th time onward, you will not be able to press the "Select" button.

When you press "Pass," the pink heart will decrease by one.

When you press "Pass" until it reaches 5 times, the hearts will decrease to 0, and you will not be able to press the "Pass" button anymore.

When you press "Pass" and "Select" until each button reaches 5 times, the game will display a popup panel summarizing the results. This panel will include information on the sweet, fatty, and salty life tubes, indicating which tubes have filled up with white color due to excess. It will also summarize in text which types of food the player chose to consume excessively.

There are a total of 8 summary screens.

Then, press the "Exit" button to leave the game, or press "Back to main menu" to press the "Start" button to enter another round of scenes including locations or mini-games. This time, head to the Quiz Place, the second location for entering mini-games, where a green "Play" button will appear.

When you press the green "Play" button, you will enter the Quiz game screen, which starts with a score of 0 displayed on the top left of the screen. The first question will appear, prompting the player to answer "Yes" or "No". Press the green "Yes" button if the answer is yes, and the red "No" button if the answer is no.

When you press the "Yes" button, the score on the top left will increase by 1 point each time.

When you press the "No" button, the score on the top left will remain the same without increasing or decreasing.

When you answer all 12 questions, a summary page will appear showing the assessment of the accuracy of daily food consumption.

There are a total of 3 summary screens.

Conclusion

The project to design and develop a game for making informed food choices based on nutritional principles stems from awareness of the importance of learning to make nutritionally balanced food choices. This aims to reduce mortality due to overweight and obesity. It advocates for changing perspectives and attitudes in accurately communicating about obesity to raise public awareness. However, there is a lack of training and dissemination of this content to the general youth due to educational personnel readiness and various other factors. This issue is close to home as daily food consumption habits have become routine. Therefore, there is a need to create tools that facilitate the transfer of skills and knowledge in an accessible and engaging format. Thus, designing and developing a learning game is chosen as the method for this project.

After summarizing the approach to carrying out this project, the project team began researching information about software or tools used in game development. After comparing various tools and conducting research, Unity was selected for game development. Unity was chosen because it is a beginner-friendly game development program that is easy to use and offers high flexibility.

Problems and Solutions

Due to the critical importance of My Health Meal game being able to effectively convey knowledge on making nutritionally balanced food choices in an easily understandable and enjoyable format, maintaining alignment and balance between storyline and gameplay mechanics has been a recurring challenge in the project's design phase. Overloading the game with nutritional content could potentially diminish its entertainment value, while too little information may not sufficiently educate players on proper food choices. Therefore, achieving a balance between nutritional education and engaging gameplay remains crucial. In addition to narrative design challenges, there have also been issues encountered in creating art assets and graphics, such as managing artwork borders and tools for seamless character animations to maintain natural movement perspectives.

Suggestion

  • The current version of My Healthy Meal game doesn't offer many locations or mini-games to choose from, and each type of food may have more than one flavor. Therefore, there may be additions to allow each type of food to have more than one flavor.
  • Creating a database to store scores, such as which players have completed the Restaurant game without exceeding any energy tubes, for the highest number of rounds, etc., will help stimulate challenge among players.
  • Choose foods that balance the amount of sweetness, fat, and salt types to avoid players having the opportunity to choose foods with one type of sweetness, fat, or salt more than others.
  • The colors of the sweetness, fat, and salt indicators may not currently convey or align with the meaning of the sweet, fat, and salt levels. Therefore, they should be clearly specified in easily understandable terms, and should include numerical units or units of measurement for each indicator, so that players can clearly understand how much of each energy type they have selected in their food choices.
  • The questions should be revised to include both correct and incorrect food consumption options mixed together, so that players do not simply answer with just "Yes" or "No".
  • The game "My Healthy Meal" still lacks fun, which can be added. For example, adding exercise activities. In the summary page of the Restaurant game, in addition to informing players about which foods they have consumed excessively, suggestions can be added regarding which types of food should be consumed instead. It might also encourage players to engage in exercise activities to reduce the intake of sweet, fat, or salt.
  • Add character animations such as jumping or dying.
